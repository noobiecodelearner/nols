About

I'm Naeem Hossain. I work with machine learning systems and write to understand how they behave once they leave controlled settings and interact with the world.

My work sits between theory and practice. I'm interested in how models generalize, how and why they fail, and how design choices—data collection, objectives, infrastructure, and human feedback—shape what systems ultimately learn. Many of the most important failures in machine learning are not algorithmic; they are systemic.

This blog is a place to think in public about those systems. The essays here are not tutorials or product advice. They are attempts to reason clearly about learning systems as evolving entities: how they age, how they accumulate technical and conceptual debt, and how their behavior changes over time.

I use this space to connect ideas from machine learning, statistics, systems design, and the social context models operate in. Writing is part of how I test and refine these ideas.
